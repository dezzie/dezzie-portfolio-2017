Hai

I like thinking, eating, and taking stuff apart. Sometimes this happens in front of a computer, sometimes in front of a stove.

It is because of the underground trance music scene in Southern California that I made my first website when I was 14, and my on-again, off-again romance with design began. You should ask me about that story sometime.

In college, I majored in psychology so I could learn how people think and feel, and why they do the things they do. I dropped a pre-med track for a Computer Applications major, because I realized that, if I was skipping class to write code instead, I might as well get some credit for it. I didn't fully take the hint though, until I went on and started a PhD in psychology, and became frustrated with an inability to find a creative outlet or take a technology-driven approach to solving problems.

Then I discovered the field of user experience while working as a Genius. I had to watch people navigate through Mac OS X for hours a day, and began to notice patterns of failure and success, frustration and delight. Suddenly, the design, technology, and psych made sense together. I quit the PhD. Soon after, I would meet Audrey Chen, who convinced me that design could truly help people and work for the common good. The rest is history.
